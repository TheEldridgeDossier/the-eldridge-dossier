EXHIBIT A

The Stow Café Defense

Jan 2026

When ICE detained the owner of a popular café in Stow, Senator Eldridge took to Facebook within hours to publicly champion the family and announce he was working to keep the man in Massachusetts.

What he never mentioned: the man had a documented criminal history including indecent assault and battery on a child, kidnapping, assault, and battery. He had admitted to sufficient facts in Concord District Court. A judge had explicitly warned him that his plea could affect his immigration status.

No statement for the victims. No acknowledgment of the charges. Only performance for an audience.

This was not a misunderstanding. This was a sitting state senator choosing the optics of immigrant advocacy over the assault victims in his own district. Twice in one news cycle.

EXHIBIT B

The Performance Economy

Ongoing

Open the senator's Facebook page. Count the milkshake selfies. Count the artisan bread reviews. Count the gala photos from Cambridge. Count the lengthy essays celebrating immigrant business owners by name while local entrepreneurs go uncelebrated.

Then count the substantive policy wins for the district he represents. Property tax relief? Housing affordability? Economic development for working families? Meaningful action on the cost of living crisis crushing his constituents?

Four hundred and eighty-six photos. One like on a mint milkshake. A career measured in selfies.

This is not governance. This is a personal brand maintained at public expense for the benefit of a narrow audience that mistakes visibility for service.

EXHIBIT C

Why He's Still Here

Structural

It is not because his constituents love him. It is because the system was built to protect him. Gerrymandered district lines. One-party dominance. A loyal base of aging true believers who reflexively heart-react every milkshake selfie and will not be around forever.

Strip away those structural advantages and Senator Eldridge would have been gone a decade ago. He knows it. That is why he responds to genuine criticism with racism accusations, hostility claims, and coffee-shop redirects rather than substance — because substance has never been required of him.

The protection is eroding. The base is dying off. The clock is ticking and he can feel it.
